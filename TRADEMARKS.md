# Trademark Policy

This policy explains how the project's **name** and **branding** may be used. It
is separate from, and additional to, the software license.

## Code license vs. marks

The source code in this repository is licensed under **GPL-3.0-or-later**
(see [`LICENSE`](./LICENSE)). That license grants you broad rights to use,
study, modify, and redistribute the **code**.

The GPL does **not** grant any rights to the project **name** or its
**logo/branding**. Names, logos, and other brand assets are *not* licensed
under the GPL and are governed by this policy instead. Copyright and trademark
are distinct: a free-software code license does not transfer control of the
marks.

## The marks

For this policy, "the marks" means:

- the project name ("Download Manager", the working public name), and
- any associated logo, icon, wordmark, or other brand assets.

The marks are (or will be) controlled by the project owner. Any registered
trademarks and the precise legal owner are **owner-supplied placeholders** and

## What you may do

- **Nominative / fair use.** You may use the name to refer to this project
  truthfully — for example, "compatible with Download Manager", "a fork of
  Download Manager", or in reviews, articles, and documentation. This does not
  imply endorsement or affiliation.
- **Unmodified redistribution.** You may redistribute unmodified official
  builds under the project name, provided you do not misrepresent their origin.

## What requires a different name

If you **fork or substantially modify** the code and distribute it, you must
use a **different name and different branding** so users are not confused about
the source or official status of the software — unless the mark owner grants
you written permission. In particular:

- Do not name your fork or derivative in a way that suggests it is the official
  project or is endorsed by the mark owner.
- Do not reuse the project's logo or brand assets on your derivative.

This restriction is on the marks only. Your rights to the **code** under
GPL-3.0-or-later are unaffected — you keep every freedom the GPL grants,
including the freedom to redistribute your modified version under a name of
your own choosing.
